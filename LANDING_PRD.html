PawPath Landing Page

Status  Draft v1 Date  May 2026 Author  Faris Rakha Scope  Route: / (root)
01 — Context

Why this page exists

Right now PawPath has no dedicated landing page. The root route drops visitors straight into the browse catalog. That works for users who already know what PawPath is — but it gives newcomers no reason to trust the platform, no orientation, and no emotional hook before asking them to scroll through animals.

The landing page is the first thing a person sees when they arrive from Instagram, TikTok, or a Google search. It needs to answer one question in under five seconds: "Is this worth my attention?" — and then move them into the catalog as fast as possible.

Key constraint: The page must not slow down the path to browsing. No quiz walls, no forced sign-up, no multi-step onboarding. The job of the landing page is to earn enough trust that clicking "Jelajahi" feels obvious.

02 — Decisions Captured

What we agreed on

These choices shape every section below. Change one and the whole page architecture shifts.

Question Decision Implication
Traffic source Instagram / TikTok + Google search Two different mental states on arrival. Social = emotionally warm, already likes animals. Search = intent-driven, comparing options. Hero must satisfy both.
Primary CTA Browse pets immediately No quiz wall before the catalog. The match profile is a feature, not a gate. Every CTA on this page goes to /jelajahi.
Audience scope Adopters only (shelters = separate page later) 100% of copy speaks to the person who wants a pet. No "Are you a shelter?" split in the nav.
Main objection "Which pet suits me?" The Profil Kecocokan feature is the answer. It must appear as a solution, not a filter UI. Frame it as "we figure that out for you."
Emotional tone Warm & story-driven Lead with a real animal's story or a human moment. Not generic "adopt don't shop" mission copy. Specific beats general.
Process detail Out of scope for this page No step-by-step adoption funnel section. Trust is built through animal cards and verified shelter badges, not a process diagram.
03 — User Entry Journeys

Who lands here and why

The landing page must serve two distinct entry states. Both share the same destination (the catalog), but they arrive with different questions in their heads.

Social visitor Instagram / TikTok
Arrives from A post or reel of a cute shelter animal, bio link
Mental state Emotionally warm. Already cares about animals. Low intent but high openness.
First question "Oh, this is where I can actually adopt that kind of animal?"
Blocker Doesn't know if they're "ready." Will leave if the page feels clinical or complicated.
What wins them A specific animal story, warmth, and a frictionless first step.
Search visitor Google
Arrives from Query like "adopsi kucing Jakarta" or "shelter anjing Jabodetabek"
Mental state Active intent. Already decided they want a pet. Evaluating options.
First question "Is this platform legit, and does it have animals near me?"
Blocker Uncertainty about whether shelters are real and active. Will bounce to competitor if trust signals are missing.
What wins them Verified shelter badges, real animals with locations, fast path to browse.

Design principle from this: The hero section must work for both. Warm enough for the social visitor, credible enough for the search visitor. The animal photo + match feature combination achieves both — emotion from the photo, trust from the "verified" language.

04 — Page Goal & Success Metrics

What success looks like

The landing page has one primary job and two supporting jobs.

Primary goal: Get the visitor to click into the browse catalog (/jelajahi) within their first session — ideally within 30 seconds of arriving.

Primary metric
CTR → /jelajahi
% of landing page visitors who click any CTA that leads to the catalog. Target: >55%.
Secondary metric
Bounce rate
% who leave without any interaction. Target: under 40% for social traffic, under 30% for search.
Tertiary metric
Match profile fills
% of visitors who fill the profil kecocokan from the landing page CTA. Target: >15%.

01 — Hero Above fold
Purpose Answer "what is this?" in under 5 seconds and earn the scroll. The emotional hook lives here.
Layout Mosaic panel grid — not a traditional split. 4 equal-width columns, each with a distinct solid-color background. Column 1 (coral bg): headline text + CTA + social proof badge. Columns 2–4: one animal photo per panel, face-cropped, photo fills the panel. The panels sit flush edge-to-edge with no gap. Full viewport height above fold on desktop. On mobile: stack to 2×2 grid, text column on top spanning full width.
Panel colors Col 1: --primary coral bg, white text. Col 2: near-black --ink bg. Col 3: --accent honey bg. Col 4: warm off-white. Each color block gives each animal its own visual identity. Avoid two adjacent panels with the same hue.
Photo direction Animals photographed against a neutral/colored studio backdrop (not lifestyle settings). Head and shoulders only — faces fill each panel. Three different animals, ideally two dogs and one cat or vice versa. Photos should feel like portraits, not snapshots.
Social proof badge Floating white pill, positioned at the bottom of the headline panel overlapping into the next panel. Content: small avatar stack (3 overlapping circles) + "120+ hewan menunggu". Subtle drop shadow. Reference: PUPJOY hero badge.
Headline Two-line. Display font (Fraunces). Line 1 plain, Line 2 coral italic. See copy below.
Primary CTA Coral filled button → /jelajahi. Label: "Temukan hewanmu". Full width on mobile.
Secondary CTA Ghost/text link below: "Atau isi profil kecocokan dulu →" — for visitors who feel the "which pet suits me?" anxiety before they browse.
Headline option A (product-forward) Hewan yang menunggu
rumah seperti milikmu.
Headline option B (story-forward) Ada yang sedang menunggu
seseorang seperti kamu.
Subheadline (same for both) Anjing dan kucing dari shelter terverifikasi di Jabodetabek. Isi profil kecocokan dan kami carikan yang paling pas.

Copy rule: The subheadline must mention "terverifikasi" and "Jabodetabek" — these two words answer both visitor types in one sentence. Social visitor: warmth from the rest. Search visitor: credibility from those two words.

Purpose Catch visitors who scrolled through without clicking anything. Warm close, one button, no email gate, no friction.

No email capture here. The CTA should be "go browse" — not "sign up for updates." An email form adds friction at the exact moment when momentum is highest. Capture comes naturally when they apply for an animal.

06 — Design Language

Visual system derived from references

These rules apply to every surface of the landing page. They are extracted from the design references provided (PUPJOY and Vetic) and adapted to PawPath's existing color tokens and font stack. They do not override the global design system — they extend it for the landing page context.

Typography

ElementSpecReference
Section headings Large, bold, uppercase. Fraunces Variable (font-display). Two-line two-tone: line 1 in --ink, line 2 in --primary coral. Treat the color break as a design choice, not an afterthought — the coral line should always be the emotionally loaded line. PUPJOY: "BRING HOME YOUR / PERFECT COMPANION"
Section labels Above every section heading: a small coral dot (•) followed by small-caps uppercase label text in --muted-foreground. Example: • Cara kerjanya. Font size ~11px, letter-spacing generous. This is a consistent system across all sections — never omit it. Vetic: "• Prevention", "• Testimonials", "• Contact"
Body copy Geist Variable, regular weight, ~15–16px, line-height ~1.65. Never uppercase. Max width ~520px to prevent runaway line lengths in centered layouts. —
CTA buttons Pill-shaped (border-radius: 999px), not rectangular. Filled coral on light sections, filled white on the coral banner. Pair with a ghost/text secondary action when needed. Reference: Vetic's pill CTA with icon. Vetic hero CTA, PUPJOY buttons

Color application

SurfaceTreatment
Page backgroundWarm off-white (--background). Sections alternate between this and pure white to create visual rhythm without heavy borders.
Hero panelsEach panel gets a distinct solid fill: coral (--primary), near-black (--ink), honey (--accent), warm off-white. No gradient, no image overlays — solid color only so the animal photo reads cleanly.
Step cardsPastel variants of the brand palette: light salmon (coral at 15% opacity), light mint (a complementary green), light honey (accent at 20% opacity). Never the full saturated color.
Final CTA bannerFull --primary coral. Only section on the page with this treatment. Makes it visually distinct and terminates the scroll with energy.

Photo treatment

RuleDetail
Studio-style backgroundsHero panel photos and featured animal cards use animals photographed against solid-color or clean neutral backdrops — not lifestyle/environment photos. The colored panel or card background IS the environment. Lifestyle photos (animals in homes, parks) are reserved for the shelter promise section mosaic only.
Rounded cornersAll photo elements use rounded corners. Hero panels: 0 (they bleed edge-to-edge). Feature cards: ~12px. Promise mosaic photos: ~16px. Never hard-cornered rectangles on standalone photos.
Face-forward croppingFor hero panels and featured cards, crop to show the animal's face and chest only. The animal should appear to be looking at or near the camera. Reference: every pet photo in PUPJOY's hero and breed grid.

White card containers (Vetic pattern)

The problem/solution step cards and any floating UI elements (social proof badge, stat callouts) sit inside white rounded-corner containers on the off-white page background. Border-radius ~20px, subtle box-shadow (0 2px 16px rgba(0,0,0,0.06)). This lifts content off the page without using heavy borders.

What to avoid: Do not let the landing page look like the catalog page with extra sections. The catalog is functional UI. The landing page is editorial. Bigger type, more whitespace, bolder color use, fewer data points per block.

07 — Copy Principles

Voice rules for this page

These apply to every line of copy on the landing page, on top of the global brand voice in CLAUDE.md.

Rule Why Example
Use specific animals, not abstract concepts Warm & story-driven tone. "Miko sedang menunggu" lands harder than "Temukan hewan peliharaan." Bad: "Ribuan hewan membutuhkan rumah." Good: "Miko sudah 3 bulan di shelter Jakarta Selatan."
Never use "peliharaan" in hero copy The word frames the animal as a possession, not a companion. It cheapens the emotional register. Bad: "Temukan peliharaan idealmu." Good: "Temukan hewanmu."
Lead with the visitor, not the platform Copy that starts with "PawPath adalah..." puts the brand first. Visitors don't care yet. Start with their reality. Bad: "PawPath menghubungkan kamu dengan..." Good: "Sedang mencari hewan yang cocok untuk apartemen kamu?"
No em dash ( — ) or bullet lists in body copy Per global brand voice rules — both are AI writing tells and break conversational flow. Rewrite as real sentences instead.
CTA labels are actions, not commands "Temukan hewanmu" feels like an invitation. "Cari Sekarang" feels like an instruction. Use: Temukan hewanmu, Mulai jelajahi, Isi profil kecocokan. Avoid: Cari Sekarang, Klik Di Sini, Daftar.
08 — Navigation

Landing page nav vs app nav

The landing page uses the same global nav component. No special landing-page-only nav. The existing nav already has "Jelajahi" and "Lamaranku" — both are correct for this page.

One consideration: The current nav has no marketing-level anchor links (e.g. scroll to "how it works"). Since we're not building a long marketing page with anchors, this is fine. If the page grows sections like "for shelters" later, revisit.

09 — Out of Scope (v1)

What this page deliberately does not include

  • Email capture / newsletter signup — adds friction with no clear conversion path yet
  • Step-by-step adoption process explainer — process varies by shelter; landing page should not over-promise a specific flow
  • Shelter onboarding CTA ("Daftarkan sheltermu") — shelters get their own page later
  • Testimonial carousel — real testimonials don't exist yet; placeholder copy creates false trust
  • Video hero — production cost vs. gain not justified for v1; revisit after traffic data exists
  • Blog / article section — SEO content strategy is a separate initiative
  • FAQ section — no evidence visitors have specific questions before browsing; add only if bounce rate data shows it's needed
